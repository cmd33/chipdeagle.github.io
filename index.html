about me

Hey internet, I'm Joe Diment.

I am currently a second year Software Engineering major at the Rochester Institute of Technology in Rochester, NY.

As a child, I had an innate curiosity and hunger for knowledge that never quite left me. I first used a computer before my third birthday and it didn't take very long for me to become an enthusiast.

As I grew older, I found a passion for video games. In order to fix the problems that would inevitably pop up from excessive computer use, I used my curiosity and passion for learning to diagnose, repair, and learn as much about the systems that I used as possible. Naturally as I progressed through my teens, I decided to choose computing as my career choice. I built my first PC at 15 and the rest is history. Software development was the logical next step. In computing, I get to "make things work."

Now, at 22 years old, I am currently looking for a full time position in order to fully apply the skills I have learned to the development of software systems.

professional skills


I have used and am proficient in a wide array of technologies. From desktop applications to web development, I am confident that my skills are suitable to the project.

I prefer to work on the back-end, but don't let that fool you. I can work on the entire stack. Python is my strongest language, and I have extensive experience with its libraries and frameworks like Django and Flask. I am also fluent with C# and many of the common technologies found in the Microsoft .NET ecosystem, such as Entity Framework and ASP.NET. Due to my exposure to web technologies, I am also proficient in JavaScript/jQuery and JavaScript libraries like React, HTML5, and CSS. I am no stranger to databases, and have experience with both SQL databases of different varieties and NoSQL databases. I am strong in Java and C.

In addition to my technical qualifications, I am also experienced with software development processes. I have served as a Scrum master on an Agile development team in a class setting as well as a project manager on a plan-driven team. I spent a year contributing on an enterprise team, and served as a peer mentor to engineers with a less robust skillset than mine. In a team setting, I can adapt to fill any role.
